Human villages like the tutorial village have wooden village buildings, sometimes including an armory shop. They are inhabited by human tribe loyalists sometimes led by a knight, and can be defended by knights and archers, who protect peasants, children, donkeys, horses, cows, and pigs. Larger village have names.


There are several different types of lone human cottages dotted around the maps. They are usually inhabited by peasants, horses, donkeys, cows, pigs, goats, and children, and some of the inhabitants can mount the horses and donkeys. Crops are typically seen growing around them and they occasionally have a guardian witchman. Some buildings may have a cellar. 


Eskimo cottages are snowy locations that has no crops and are inhabited by Eskimos and Eskimo children. An Eskimo village is a small snow village with a few buildings and is also inhabited by more Eskimos and Eskimo children. You may find the occasional snowman. There are also Nomad cottages in deserts made with sandstone walls and nomads typically grow no crops. You find nomads, goats, camels, and nomad children. A nomad village is similar to a normal human village, but has the sandstone wall structure with no crops and nomadic people and animals. A native village is a small village with tribe loyalties to humans. It is inhabited by natives and native children.


Blacksmiths and artisan Legends, can be defended by knights and archers. Located in a town setting, with a forge. They sometimes have cellars. Lone Guard Posts that appear on maps are defended by knights and archers, who own cows, donkeys, and dogs. There is normally a second floor above some stairs. Warriors often inhabit a castle-like location with wooden structures, led by a shaman. Located in a town setting, often has a gold treasure chest stockpile. Can trigger when you build a throne. New warriors tend to immigrate. When they get triggered they go for your keeper but sometimes accept ransoms. Some humans normally choose to live alone like witches (Located in a wood cottages with tribe loyalties to monsters, brewing potions).


Knights typically live in castles, led by a duke, who is defended by the knights, archers, and priests, as well as sometimes by the unique character Father Oblivion. Peasants, children, donkeys, cows, and pigs are often in the area. Expect a shop and expect to get attacked if you build a throne for yourself or display impaled heads. They usually receive regular new recruits from the surrounding areas. Teutons are castle dwellers that like brick structures, inhabited by human tribe loyalists, led by a teuton boss, and defended by teutons and friars. Located in a town setting, expect a gold treasure chest stockpile.


Elementalists tend to live in brick towers and have human tribe loyalties. The elementalist is a leader and he is usually surrounded by his elementals. He can attack and tends to stand at a distance and let his servants do the fighting.


Bandits are usually Cave dwellers with a bandit leader, bandit fighters and their own bandit tribe loyalties. They can surplant a kobold cave or a lawful human cottage. They may ambush, get greedy for gold, attack to steal gold, and demand ransoms. Desert thieves live in sandstone-walled castles in the desert biome, with a leader and fighters including thieves and a unique sorcerer. They ride camels and can ambush, triggered by anger at a throne or an impaled head, they will attack to kill enemy leaders. They stockpile gold in treasure chests, have furniture, and mount beasts. Immigrants are mostly other thieves who can fight. Djinn reside in the sandstone maze, where magic lamps can sometimes be found.


Goblin cottages exist, someimes having supplanted an elven cottage. Larger groups can be led by a troll and have bandit tribe loyalties. They may have extra melee, spell, and archery training and may ambush. Goblin settlements often mimic human settlements but you also find goblin caves. The Goblin Cave is a dungeon location inhabited by goblin warriors with tribe loyalties to greenskins. The cave often has furniture, and may recieve immigration of goblin warriors with fighter traits. A network of goblin caves can exist underground and are a string of Goblin Caves, with multiple levels that get more challenging as the player progresses deeper into the caves. These caves can be led by a troll.


The largest goblin settlements can be led by the goblin king and populated by goblin warriors, guards, wizards, archers, priests, and kaboomers, as well as a troll. These larger locations typically contain furniture and the inhabitants may ambush when they get greedy for gold. The attack behavior is often to steal gold and they may ask for ransoms. The locations receive immigration from other greenskins over time and can be built on displaced dwarven villages.


Dwarf settlements can be dwarf Vaults, which exist in z-level locations below your base, having dwarf tribe loyalties and populated by dwarves with fighter traits. Other Dwarf Caves can be mine town dungeons inhabited by dwarves led by a dwarf leader, who stockpiles minerals and built their mine in a place surrounded by natual resources. Dwarf fighters with increasing skill in melee combat are found deeper down into z-levels. The dwarves are triggered to attack when provoked or stolen from. Some of your outlaw Minions aggravate the dwarf tribes but they can accept often ransoms to call off the attack. The most grand dwarven location is the huge minetown dungeon led by a dwarf baron and featuring furniture, stockpiles, chests and a shop.


Various z-levels include rat caves and bat z-levels, vaults occupied by the respective monster types. The rat caves can be home to rat fighters, led by a rat king. Lava Z-levels can be ruled by a red dragon leader using fire elementals as fighters. A
Z-level zombie castle, is infested by swarmer zombies. It can be a challenging location to navigate due to the zombie hordes. Black dragons can exist in a castle with a layout similar to dwarves, but with a black dragon occuptant that has suplanted them. Balrog z-levels are castle-like with a balrog leader and loyalties to other monsters. It is populated by trolls, goblin warriors, and goblin archers being dangerous to explore. Krakens z-levels are likely to be challenging due to the powerful kraken fighters present. Some z-levels are plain empty.


The common elves in the game are represented by elven cottages located in forest biomes, which are made of wood and populated by elf tribe loyalists. A large elf settlement will be led by an elf lord. The cottages are furnished, and the inhabits include deadly archers, non-fighter elf children, and animals.


There are also dark elf locations, including the Dark Elf Caves, which are mine town dungeons that house dark elves led by warriors. The dark elves are triggered to attack when provoked or stolen from by outlaws, and they stockpile minerals but can sometimes accept ransoms for temporary peace. The fighters in these location are warriors who improve their melee combat skills as you move deeper into the z-levels.


More powerful dark elven allies and enemies are located in minetown dungeons, which are usually given a friendly name at the bottom of a few z-levels with dungeon furniture. They include recruitable dark elf warriors, a dark elf lord, dark elf children, and have pet rats. They have an immigration system to accept new elves into the tribe. The entrance can be a little difficult to discover and may not be marked on the map.


The gnomes are located in a minetown dungeon and their tribe is loyal to itself but peaceful to many. Their leader is a gnome chief and the inhabitants consist of gnomes that can sometimes fight but never choose to invade. The gnomes tend to have a level connection to some layers of dungeons. The settlement at the bottom will be a mine town and is usually given a name for a town far underground. Their shops have useful equipment. 


Lizardman tribes comes in different sizes. Lizardmen live in forest cottages, which can surplant elven cottages. They are a tribe loyal to other lizardmen. The smaller lizardman tribes may live in a mud cottages in village-like surroundings. They are loyal to their tribe and are primarily fighters, consisting only of lizardmen. The large lizardmen tribes are located in desert biomes and live in big mud villages led by a lizard lords. They are also loyal to their tribe and consist exclusively of lizardmen fighters. The location usually has a town name and has good mushrooms for sale in their shops. They have furniture and village surroundings. Both small and large tribes may ambush and are triggered by impaled heads. They hate expanding keepers and their powers. They get angry when attacked and when a keeper is weak, or are stolen from or aggravated by outlaws in the player's faction. They attack enemy leaders and sometimes are seen having taken over a village of elves. Immigrant lizardman who fight are relatively common.


Various animal dens exist. Mostly in the mountains. A Wolf Den is usually a cave inhabited by wolves and sometimes a feral child, often with bandit tribe loyalties. There are also bat caves and cave Bear Dens. Ants come in different shapes and sizes and their nests may range from being small and closed off to being a dungeon location led by an ant queen and populated by ant workers and soldiers. Open ant nests may have surplanted other settlements, while others are simply a mining hazard. Black Rats tend not to fight with ants but they do fight and often carry plagues, they may not be marked on the map. They can also be built on the bones of other settlements and be surrounded by natural ores. 


Some monsters live in isolation. The Giant Spider Caves can be found in various cave layouts, full of webs, and can take over dwarf or kobold caves, posing a challenge for players. The Cyclops Cave is a hostile location inhabited by a mushroom-collecting creature that attacks faction members, triggered by large populations or outlaws. The Yeti location is a hostile cave system inhabited by a yeti, hostile to almost everything, and requires players to be prepared for fighting. The Hydra Swamps, surrounded by a thick fog, are found in the swamp biome and are populated by a hydra, requiring players to be prepared for combat. Kraken can be found in Mountain Lakes, monsters with uniquely long tentacles. The Minotaur location is a challenging maze-like system made with brick and furniture (if you can find it) inhabited by a fearsome minotaur, but offering good rewards for those who explore it.


The Red, Black, and Green Dragon caves are extremely dangerous locations inhabited by powerful dragons that pose a significant threat to anyone who enters or tries to steal from them. White dragon lairs are found in cave glaciers that resemble a dungeon. They have similarities to other dragon locations, within snow biomes. The inhabitants include a white dragon leader and white naga fighters. The location is connected to the white dragon entry on the top level with lower levels. Yellow dragons are found exclusively in dungeons.


There are other small tribes of creatues loyal to each other in various places. Adamantium Golems are fighters and pose a huge mining hazard, often located in isolated areas surrounded by extra resources such as adamantium ore. The Unicorn Herd is a location nestled within a forest, led by a the unicorn leader and populated by fellow unicorns allowing new unicorns to immigrate into their herd.


Darkshrine is a castle-like location that serves as the domain of the demon race, led by the demon lord. It has a foreboding atmosphere, with an entryway inhabited by unconquerable ghosts leading upwards to the throne room. The demons residing in the den are skilled fighters and can demand ransoms. Other demons immigrate into the den. They are easily triggered by demon shrines.


Ent woods and Dryad woods are forest locations filled with roaming spirits. Cemetery locations with a random layout exist, populated by zombies with an entranced marked by furniture like graves. Harpy Caves have greenskin loyalties. Vampire Vaults are dungeon locations. The Sokoban Puzzle is located in an island vault door dungeon and is populated by dark keepers. The Holy Temples and Evil Temples are temple locations with altars.

The statue of Adoxie's Feet can been found in the base map. Below is a devil in a cave with stockpiles of gold and treasure chests. Rat people are loyal to the rat king and can attack triggered by anger. Wizard towers are located in forest biomes and have a tribe loyal to bandits. Dark mages can live in a minetown dungeon and have a tribe loyal to dark elves. The leader is a keeper mage, with fighters including goblins, trolls, priests, wizards, vampires, and special creatures like a legendary human and harpies. They can be triggered by anger at a throne or impaled head. They too stockpile gold and minerals and have furniture.


The final dungeon is a significant end game location that features an Adoxie temple with a leader Adoxie priest, abomination fighters, and spider food (tied up victims). The dungeon has different levels with varying enemies, including the main enemy in Adoxie's temple. Shallow dungeons are random, unmarked locations with a variety of furniture and items such as dungeon goods. Inhabitants include bandits, cyclopes, witches, golems of various types, elementals, gnomes, dwarves, and various small animals like bats, snakes, spiders, flies, and rats. Deeper dungeon levels typically contain stronger monsters than shallow dungeons, including green dragons and various types of golems and elementals. They may contain unique monsters like shapeshifters and are often very dangerous. The Deepest Dungeons are the most dangerous of all, containing powerful monsters like red and yellow dragons, and special furnishings like bleeding totems.


The Black Market (located in a hidden location) is essentially a market for illicit goods and contains a variety of items like amulets, scrolls, potions, and materials. The black market has guards to protect its wares and may be triggered to attack if stolen from. The entry to the black market is a secret.


Death's lair is a location containing the spirit of death, with loyalties to other monsters. Past death, if you find a way, hell is a hidden location with devil and spirit fighters, and loyalties to other monsters, along with hell furniture. It is a far end game location, indicating its difficulty level. Satan, not marked on the map, is leader of hell with devil fighter inhabitants. Some players claim to have travelled all the way through hell to the outback.


There is still some legacy content in the vanilla files that is not currently used. The orc village was a brick structure with tribe loyalties to the Greenskin race and populated by orcs and ogres. It reflected a typical village with furniture and surroundings, and could surplant a dwarves village. The orc cave was a dungeon structure with tribe loyalties to bandits and populated by orcs who got greedy for gold. It could surplant a kobold cave and had immigrants with fighter traits. Cottage orcs were similar to orc caves and could surplant an elven cottage. The ogre cave was a dungeon structure with tribe loyalties to the Greenskin race and populated by ogres with fighter traits. It reflected an outside village with immigration capabilities for ogres with fighter traits.
